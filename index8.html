♠   Union Hall   ♠





In this Union Hall project, I continue to perceive things from a naive kid, to explore the combination of ancient and modern, pay attention to details and the needs of both humans and nature, and focus on playing with one simple geometric figure. put this one simple shape into different sizes in one building.



The overall shape of this building is inspired by the Chicago-style window, a window with three pieces for air circulation. The building has two floors, the first floor is a gallery with cafe on two sides, the second floor is the meeting space with offices and terrace on two sides. On the outside is a small plaza for installation art and sculptures.



The first floor is mostly for the public, and the entrances are on two sides. Art students and teachers can exhibit their work in here. The second floor is a relatively private space for people who belong to the union to have meeting and conference here, the entrance is on the back of the building.
